Showreel

Non-fiction Animation short film project

A short film based around the theme of "Procrastination". Made using Substance Designer as a compositor to create the inked-out style. By rendering out passes such as Normal and Depth I could manipulate the various depth information in SD to create complex levels of ink style. The Substance Graph's many exposed parameters could be animated with the $TIME value to add the extra level of uneasy distortion to the imagery. Substance Player allowed me to run the various individual passes as sequences through it and get unique final elements to be layered on top of each other. Here is another example of the same technique used for a test project

Terrain R&D

Research and development that lead to my 3-step semi-procedural workflow to create visibly distinct terrains. Most of the unique-ness comes from what general shapes are used to create a heightmap in Substance Designer. This heightmap is exported in a high quality to a Houdini heightfield simulation where a lot of the natural grooves and plateaus are formed. To fix the "flat" feeling of a 2D texture being simulated in another 2D environment I then displace the mesh terrain using vector displacement or (a heavier method) VDB advection. This gives the indents and more natural curvature to the terrain.

Viking Story environment & character Concept

For the environments in this project I tried pushing myself to manipulate assets based on the camera position. I knew the final result was going to a flatter 2D version so I worked around stretching, sculpting and generally deforming terrain so that to the camera would naturally be bending towards a point of focus

The bridges are a type of penrose triangle that shouldn't physically make sense. A lot of time was spent on the lighting and modelling as it was hard to keep consistent when the 3D geometry did not make logical sense outside of on orthographic perspective.
